A book and webpage created for Christopher Bolton’s From Ground Zero to Degree Zero. Inspired from the post-apocalyptic imagery of Akira (1990), I used risograph prints & halftone textures to create contrast between imagery and bold typography. Book is bound usiung a Japanese stab bind. Printed on 110g cream paper. Website hosted on GitHub.

The typeface specimen website uses a two column grid structure to mirror the two key weight system of the typeface.

Select pages from the book

All images were printed using a risograph printer while the text was printed using an inkjet printer. Getting the registration right was a nightmare but the contrast of the crisp typography to the soft texture of the risograph images was worth the effort!

Drying risograph prints
Stab binding in action
Final 4 editions of the book

Translating a very much textural book to the digital space was also an exciting challenge. I wanted the website to mirror the book in visuals and concept while taking advantage of moments unique to the web.
