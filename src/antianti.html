Introduction
In the summer of 2019, I had the chance to work at AntiAnti as a graphic design intern.

Due to the NDA I signed, I cannot show a bulk of the work I’ve done at AntiAnti. The project shown below is a studio project for the AntiAnti E:O:D talk series.
E:O:D
E:O:D is a design talk series curated by AntiAnti which invites designers from diverse backgrounds to have a conversation with AntiAnti about their process and ethos. Working collaboratively with the other designers at AntiAnti, I designed the identity and promotional material for the newly formed E:O:D talk series.

Final Thoughts
My Internship at AntiAnti
Working with real clients at AntiAnti, I learnt a lot about designing and delivering on a tight time constraint. This was also one of my first working experiences where I was collaborating with other designers on the same project. The studio would have each designer draft up a design for the same client and collaboratively narrow down to the best design through crits. And this would be done over and over again until the final design. This iterative process was eye opening for me and is one which I now use even in my personal projects.

Additionally by working in the heart of NYC, I not only learnt a lot from my work experience at the studio but also through experiencing the various events that were going on in New York at the time. I had a lot of fun attending talks by established designers (I still have the poster from Natasha Jen’s talk with Eye on Design hanging in my room) and checking out small exhibitions all over Brooklyn.
